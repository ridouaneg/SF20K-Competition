Short-Films 20K (SF20K) Competition

Workshop | Benchmark | arXiv | Dataset | Code

The Short-Films 20K (SF20K) Competition aims to advance story-level video understanding by leveraging the new SF20K dataset[1]. While recent multimodal models have demonstrated progress in video understanding, existing benchmarks are largely limited to short videos with simple narratives. In contrast, our competition focuses on complex, long-term reasoning in storytelling by introducing multiple-choice and open-ended question answering tasks.

Dataset

The competition is based on SF20K[1], a large-scale, publicly available dataset of 20,143 short films, amounting to 3,684 hours of video content. Each film is self-contained, lasting 5 to 40 minutes (average 11 minutes), and spans a variety of genres. SF20K includes automatically generated and manually curated question-answer (QA) pairs.

  • SF20K-Train: 19,071 movies with 191,007 automatically generated QA pairs, provided for model training.
  • SF20K-Test-Expert (public): 50 movies with 500 QA pairs, used for validation and leaderboard ranking.
  • SF20K-Test-Expert (private): 50 movies with 500 QA pairs, used for final ranking.

The dataset is available on Hugging Face, with additional annotations (shot boundaries, subtitles, face tracks) on GitHub. Videos are hosted on YouTube and Vimeo, URLs are provided.

Evaluation
  • Output type: Free-form answers, typically a short and concise sentence.
  • Evaluation metric: LLM-QA-Eval
Phases
  • Phase 1: Public Test Set
    • Methods are evaluated on the public test set, which include 50 movies, 500 open-ended questions (OEQs), and a total duration of 10 hours. The public test set is used for validation and leaderboard ranking.
  • Phase 2: Private Test Set
    • Methods are evaluated on the private test set, which include 50 movies, 500 open-ended questions (OEQs), and a total duration of 10 hours. The private test set will be released on October 3rd, 2025. Further instructions will be provided for the private test set evaluation.
